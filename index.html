Conclusion

Gandhiji was a simple person who used to mix ideals in his personality.

He wanted to see India a decveloped country, free from malpractices, diseases, poverty, discrimination and hunger.

For his dream to come true we have to leave our greed and continue to work for our country taking it as a duty of a ciizen and a return to our motherland.

12. Partnerships for the Goals

No movement in India gained succes without participation for the people. Similarly participation of the citizens of the country is necessary for these Sustainable Development Goals to be achived by India.

One should not think that he had bought the government giving his vote.

No one should hesitate in cleaning the country or thinking for the welfare of the country.

11. Peace, Justice and strong Institutions

Gandhiji was a firm believer of non-violence and wanted to have its followers keep faith in peace and Truth

On this principle of Ghandhiji, India installed a democratic setup where peace shall be maintained by the authorities and Justice provided ro the innoscent and victim.

Still there is a need to make them more powerful to implement their procedures more efficiently.

10. Responsible Consumption and Production

During the first World War crops failed in many parts of India, many farmers committed sucide and a major part from what was left in crops was used to feed the army.

Gandhiji wanted the people of the country to produce for their need and not consume in greed.

Now-a-days this need to be executed to control the greed of rich industrialists.

9. Sustainable Cities and Communities

The era of late colonial period saw Industrial Revolution. Machines used to do mass production, workers lose their jobs

Gandhiji was in favour of production by masses. He wanted the people to make and wear Swadeshi

'Swadeshi' led to the strengthening of Indian Industries which now contribute to India's economy.

8. Reduced Inequalities

The time before colonial period and during colonial period saw a massive discrimination on the basis of caste.

Gandhiji was against this discrimination and never called dalits, instead gave them the title 'Harijan'.

In the present time discrimination on the basis of caste, religion, gender or race is strictly prohibited by the constitution of India and is a punishable offence.

7. Affordable and Clean Energy

The people in those dayus uses to cook food by burning firewood.

To make India a developed country Gandhiji wanted the people of India to aquire modern methods which were much cleaner and easy to use than the old ones which pollute the environment.

Today a scheme called 'Ujjwala Yojana' has replaced several firewood with CNG gas cylinders contributing to environmental safety.

6. Clean Water and Sanitation

The Indian people during colonial period were not well-educated, they even used to go in open areas for excretion

Gandhiji always emphasized on 'Swachch Bharat' and himself cleaned the toilet to glorify the work of sweeper.

Now-a-days government is trying to provide good sanitation facilities to each section of society in every area of the country.

5. Gender Equality

Indian culture didn't give much freedom and power to women. The society was reluctant to let women work outside the house

Gandhiji encouraged women to take part in mass struggles and contribute to India's freedom struggle

Women came out of their houses participated in struggles and even went to prisons. Now-a-days men and women are equally valued.

4. Quality Education

Education was something not equally given to every section of society in a way it should be given during Colonial period.

Gandhiji used to visit many schools and taught the students the meaning of swaraj and encouraged them to work for their country

Now education is being given in an open and fair manner, free to children upto age an of 14 years.

3. Good Health and Well Being

In the days of 1900s diseases like Polio had no treatment and people with this often die.

Gandhiji wanted to make India a healthy India where no one will die of not being treated well.

In today's world campaigns against Polio, Measels, Rubella, etc. are being conducted to provide free vaccination so that it may not cause an epidemic or plague.

2.Zero Hunger

Hunger was something India suffered a lot from due to poverty and discrimination in the days of 1900s, a person who is poor or is of a very low caste suffered from hunger.

Gandhiji used to skip his one time meal so that it may be saved for the one who is poor and hungry

Now-a-days we see mid-day meals, Bhoj Banks and also government provided subsidy on food grains.

1. No Poverty

Gandhiji wanted to make India free from poverty caused by the centuries of being looted by Britishers.

Poverty was one of Gandhiji's main steps to reform society.

Still this agenda can be executed in present time. We see facilities provided to BPLs and concessions based on income etc.
